01 — About

Still Figuring It Out.

I’m a creative director and brand strategist based in Kathmandu, Nepal. I think in visuals, work across campaigns, events, and digital — and I care about making things that actually feel like something. It started with a camera. A documentary about a farmer and climate change, made by students under 18, reached 1 million people and was screened at the UN Climate Summit in Glasgow. That project taught me early that craft and conviction together travel further than either one alone. Since then I’ve designed campaigns that moved hundreds of thousands of people, built events that made national news, run social media that grew real audiences, and built my own portfolio site from scratch. I work across the full creative stack — visual design, brand positioning, content strategy, digital communications, and the kind of event direction where every touchpoint matters. I currently serve as Brand Strategy Fellow at Idea Studio Nepal, where I help Nepal’s entrepreneurship ecosystem get better at telling its own story. I’m also Founding President of the MIT Entrepreneurship Club — which I started from nothing and turned into something with a national footprint. The civic and community work is in the portfolio because it’s real. So is the film. So is the leadership. This is what range looks like when it’s backed by actual output.
